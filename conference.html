Welcome to Longevity Revolution 2026

“On behalf of the International Association of Stem Cell & Regenerative Medicine (IASRM), I extend a warm welcome to all participants of the 11th World Congress—Longevity Revolution 2026, scheduled for 22–24 May 2026, in New Delhi, India.

Our mission remains steadfast: to translate groundbreaking scientific discoveries into transformative therapies that enhance human health and longevity.

This year, the congress will unite top scientists, clinicians, and industry pioneers to explore the latest breakthroughs in anti-ageing, stem cell, regenerative, and functional medicine through a robust program of expert talks, interactive workshops, and invaluable networking opportunities (IASRM Global).”

Longevity Revolution: Transforming Health and Lifespan

The theme of this year’s congress, “Longevity Revolution: Transforming Health and Lifespan,” reflects the growing global momentum and excitement in longevity science. As we push the boundaries of what’s possible, we are reshaping our understanding of ageing and opening new frontiers for extended, healthy lifespans.

We are honoured to host this milestone event in New Delhi, India, an emerging center of excellence in stem cell and regenerative medicine. I look forward to welcoming you to what promises to be an inspiring, collaborative, and future-focused gathering.

  • Prabhu Chandra Mishra
  • M.Sc., PhD
  • Founder President, IASRM

On behalf of the International Association of Stem Cell & Regenerative Medicine (IASRM), I extend a warm welcome to all participants of the 11th World Congress, Longevity Revolution 2026.
Our mission is to promote the translation of scientific breakthroughs into innovative therapies that can improve human health and longevity.

This year’s congress, Longevity Revolution 2026, will bring together leading scientists, clinicians, and industry leaders from around the world to explore the latest advances in anti-ageing, stem cell, regenerative, and functional medicine.

The theme of this year’s congress is “Longevity Revolution: Advancing Human Health and Lifespan”. This theme reflects the growing excitement and momentum around the field of longevity science, which is transforming our understanding of ageing and opening up new possibilities for extending healthy lifespans.

Our Activities

Organizing international congresses and workshops

We provide a platform for leading experts to share their latest findings and insights through scientific presentations, networking sessions, and interactive workshops.

Offering educational courses and training programs

We develop and deliver high-quality educational resources to equip healthcare professionals with the knowledge and skills necessary to integrate stem cell therapies into their clinical practice.

Supporting research and collaborative studies

We provide funding and resources to researchers conducting promising investigations in the field of stem cell and regenerative medicine.

Advocating for policy and regulatory frameworks

We actively engage with researchers, scientists and policymakers and regulatory bodies to guide us the safe and ethical development and application of stem cell therapies.

Abstract Submission Open:
  • 15th January 2026
Abstract submission closes:
  • 31 October 2026
Abstract acceptance confirmation email:
  • 30th June 2026
Abstracts must be written in English. Abstracts that are submitted in other language will not be accepted. The title and body of the abstract must be sent in a single document in both languages in a Word format (.doc; .docx). The author is required to enter the following data in the form: name, surname, organisation, city, degree and specialisation. Title: the title, which must be written in English, should be concise and without abbreviations. The title must not exceed 150 characters. Abstract: the body of the abstract must be written in English, and must not exceed 300 words, excluding the word count for the bibliography. Abstracts made up of less than 200 words will not be accepted. Please use “Times New Roman” as font, size 12, with a line spacing of 1.5. You may include images and/or charts, and a relevant bibliography in your abstract. Please proofread the abstract carefully to make sure that there are no mistakes in typing and/or grammatical errors. Please consider that the abstract will be published as submitted. In case of studies conducted on human beings, the authors should state that the study has been approved by the Ethics Committee. The authors should also state that the research referred to in their work was conducted in accordance with the Declaration of Helsinki and the international principles governing any research involving an animal. The author may not submit more than 3 presentations. Topics When submitting an abstract, please indicate the topic associated with the abstract. You may choose one topic for each abstract. The topic indicated in the form will not be considered binding, and the Scientific Committee may change it when categorizing it within the congress sessions.
Abstracts must be submitted using the online abstract submission form. All abstracts submitted must be original work and the following must be clearly indicated: Objectives Materials and Methods Results Conclusions All abstract citations must be cited according to the AMERICAN MEDICAL ASSOCIATION (AMA) CITATION STYLE Any abstracts that have been previously presented at other congresses and/or events will not be accepted. Abstracts are subject to acceptance by the Scientific Committee, which will evaluate the work on the basis of the following criteria: Originality and relevance of the topic Scientific validity The author is required to send his/her Curriculum Vitae in the European format, which must be a signed original document including personal details (address, phone and mobile number, email). The CV must be sent in an online format through the abstract registration form. The abstracts of authors who have not submitted their CV will not be accepted.
